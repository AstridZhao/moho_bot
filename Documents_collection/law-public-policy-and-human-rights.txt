Search                      Academics    Find Your Program   Law, Public Policy and Human Rights       Law, Public Policy and Human Rights  Undergraduate   This Nexus provides both an academic and experiential lens. You will choose courses from several departments including politics, economics, history, and sociology in order to examine how both law and public policies are embedded in much larger social, historical, and economic realities.      Nexus Track Chairs           Program Overview    The goal of the Nexus in Law, Public Policy, and Human Rights is to provide you with the tools necessary to form a deep understanding of how complex relationships between local and national political processes both create public policies and shape legislation and its interpretation. Students will schedule an advising meeting with a track chair to get approval and complete a Plan of Study form to be returned to the Nexus Program office.   The sequence of a Nexus is part of what makes it unique:  In preparation for the summer internship or research, students complete courses chosen in consultation with the track chair. If seeking funding through LYNK UAF, students will additionally complete orientation and advising, and online training.   COLL-211 is taken after the internship or research project and culminates in a presentation at LEAP Symposium.
